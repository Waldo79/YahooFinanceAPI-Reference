## Market-state limitation

This evidence set was captured on Sunday, July 12, 2026. The US cash-equity
market and the reviewed European/foreign-exchange markets were closed at the
time of capture. Therefore:

- observed field values must not be assumed to represent regular-session behavior;
- bid, ask, volume, market-time, price-change, pre-market, and post-market fields
  may differ materially during active trading;
- some conditionally returned fields may appear or disappear in other market states;
- futures and cryptocurrency observations must be evaluated separately because
  those markets may be active while US cash equities are closed; and
- a later targeted capture should be run during US regular trading hours, with a
  separate active foreign-exchange observation, before assigning high stability
  confidence to market-state-sensitive fields.

The present run is valid evidence of fields observed in this Sunday snapshot,
but not complete evidence of all open-market variants.

## Important interpretation notes

- `corporateActions` was broadly present as an array, usually empty. Its child fields were observed in non-empty dividend records for both `NAC` and `PDI`.
- `corporateActions[].meta.amount` was returned as a string, not a number.
- `dividendDate` was plausible for stock observations but stale or otherwise not current for both closed-end funds. It must not be treated as the sole authoritative upcoming ex-dividend date.
- `bookValue` and `priceToBook` were observed across more than one security class, so they should not be restricted to individual stocks.
- Fund fields were conditional. Some appeared in `SPY`, `QQQI`, and `VTSAX` but were absent from `XNACX`.
- `trailingThreeMonthNavReturns` appeared in the ETF observations but not in the reviewed mutual-fund/NAV observations.
- `contractSymbol` was returned as Boolean `false` for both futures symbols, despite its name.
- `maxSupply` was `21000000` for `BTC-USD` and `0` for `ETH-USD`; zero must not automatically be interpreted as a true capped maximum.
- `firstTradeDateMilliseconds` can be negative for pre-1970 dates. The normalized output did not decode the negative `^GSPC` timestamp.
- Futures `regularMarketPreviousClose` was inconsistent with the returned price and change fields in both reviewed futures observations.
- FX bid, ask, and regular-market price values may represent different source instants; `EURUSD=X` showed a crossed bid/ask snapshot.

## Promotion rule

Before a field is added to the master field database, assign:

- canonical field and CSV names;
- Yahoo and CSV data types;
- applicable security classes;
- units and timestamp interpretation;
- nullability and expected presence;
- validation and parser rules;
- evidence symbols and market states;
- stability and evidence status; and
- any reliability or synchronization warning.

Fields should be promoted in small reviewed groups rather than all 64 at once.
