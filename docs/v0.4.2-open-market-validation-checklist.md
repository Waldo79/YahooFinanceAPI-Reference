# v0.4.2 Open-Market Validation Checklist

## Purpose

Repeat the validated Sunday capture during active trading so field presence,
values, timing, and synchronization can be compared across market states.

The Sunday evidence run was:

`2026-07-12T22-10-02.433Z_run-0001`

Do not promote fields into `data/master_field_database.csv` solely from this
comparison. Record the observations first.

## Timing

Run the capture on a normal weekday after the US cash-equity market has opened.

Prefer a time when:

- US stocks, ETFs, closed-end funds, and indexes are actively trading;
- foreign exchange is active;
- futures are active; and
- enough time has passed after the opening bell for volume and bid/ask fields
  to populate normally.

## Symbols

Use the original symbol set and add `XPDIX` so both closed-end-fund market-price
and NAV pairs are represented:

- `AAPL`
- `MSFT`
- `SPY`
- `QQQI`
- `NAC`
- `XNACX`
- `PDI`
- `XPDIX`
- `VTSAX`
- `ZT=F`
- `CL=F`
- `^GSPC`
- `^DJI`
- `BTC-USD`
- `ETH-USD`
- `EURUSD=X`
- `JPY=X`

Matched closed-end-fund pairs:

- `NAC` market price / `XNACX` end-of-day NAV
- `PDI` market price / `XPDIX` end-of-day NAV

Yahoo commonly forms a NAV companion symbol by preceding and appending `X` to
the exchange-traded symbol, but this is a naming heuristic rather than a
guaranteed universal rule.

## Before the run

- Confirm the local repository is current.
- Confirm the capture utility version is still v0.4.2.
- Confirm `XPDIX` is included in the requested symbol list.
- Do not overwrite the Sunday run folder.
- Record the local clock time and time zone.

## After the run

Confirm:

- every requested symbol has an HTTP result;
- the validation report is `PASS`;
- raw and normalized files exist for every successful result;
- the run manifest records all requested symbols;
- no cookie, crumb, or other authentication secret was persisted; and
- the new run has its own timestamped folder.

## Comparison points

Compare the weekday run with the Sunday run for:

- `marketState`;
- field presence and absence;
- `regularMarketTime`;
- `regularMarketPrice`;
- `regularMarketPreviousClose`;
- `regularMarketChange`;
- `regularMarketChangePercent`;
- bid, ask, bid size, and ask size;
- regular-market volume;
- pre-market and post-market fields;
- `sourceInterval`;
- `exchangeDataDelayedBy`;
- `corporateActions`;
- `regularMarketDayRange`;
- `hasPrePostMarketData`; and
- newly unmapped JSON paths.

## Asset-specific checks

### Stocks, ETFs, closed-end funds, and indexes

- Verify that price, previous close, change, and percent change agree.
- Check whether bid/ask and size fields become more coherent during trading.
- Check whether additional session-sensitive fields appear.
- Compare `NAC` with `XNACX` and `PDI` with `XPDIX`.
- Remember that NAV companion symbols may retain the prior end-of-day NAV until
  a new NAV is published.

### Futures

- Recheck whether `regularMarketPreviousClose` agrees with price and change.
- Recheck `contractSymbol`, especially its observed Boolean `false` value.
- Confirm contract and expiration fields remain present.

### Foreign exchange

- Recheck bid/ask ordering and whether price lies near the quoted spread.
- Treat bid, ask, and regular-market price as potentially asynchronous unless
  repeated observations show otherwise.

### Cryptocurrency

- Confirm the cryptocurrency-specific field set.
- Continue treating `maxSupply = 0` as ambiguous rather than as proof of a true
  zero maximum.

## Evidence to retain

Retain:

- the run manifest;
- the validation report;
- the unmapped-path report;
- all normalized files;
- the capture start and end times; and
- a short comparison summary identifying fields that appeared, disappeared, or
  changed type.

## Completion criterion

The open-market validation is complete when the new run passes validation and a
comparison summary has been committed without prematurely promoting uncertain
fields into the master database.
